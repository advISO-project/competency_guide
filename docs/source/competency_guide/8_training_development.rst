Using the competency framework to support training and development
===================================================================

The final component of our proposed bioinformatics competency framework
focuses on how it can be used to support ongoing staff training and
development.

The role of competency profiles is not just to evidence current skill levels
of team members, but to also provide a basis for identifying development
needs, planning training, and understanding the overall skill mix and
resilience of the team. Alongside the framework itself and the documents
used to track individual skills, teams should dedicate time to reviewing
competency records and planning training needs at both an individual and
team level.

Although the exact process will vary between organisations, several core
activities should always be considered:

.. grid:: 1
  :gutter: 3

  .. grid-item-card:: Identification and tracking of available training courses
    :class-card: sd-bg-light sd-text-dark

    Maintaining an up-to-date list of internal and external training
    opportunities ensures staff can access relevant development options.

  .. grid-item-card:: Identification of skills gaps within the workforce
    :class-card: sd-bg-light sd-text-dark

    Reviewing competency records helps highlight areas where individuals or
    the wider team require further development.

  .. grid-item-card:: Resilience planning and understanding team skill mix
    :class-card: sd-bg-light sd-text-dark

    Mapping skills across the team supports succession planning and ensures
    critical tasks are covered.

  .. grid-item-card:: Wider workforce planning
    :class-card: sd-bg-light sd-text-dark

    Aggregated training needs and skills gaps can inform recruitment
    decisions and long-term workforce strategy.

  .. grid-item-card:: Collective team training needs
    :class-card: sd-bg-light sd-text-dark

    Some development needs are shared across the team and are best addressed
    through group training rather than individual courses.
